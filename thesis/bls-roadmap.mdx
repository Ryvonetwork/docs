---
title: "BLS Roadmap"
description: "Why BLS matters for Agon later, but is not the current thesis."
---

BLS is a future scaling unlock, not the foundation of the thesis.

Today, Agon already demonstrates:

- latest-commitment compression
- bundle settlement compression
- cooperative clearing rounds

Those wins do **not** require BLS.

What BLS changes later is the density of cooperative rounds:

- fewer signature bytes
- more participants per round
- more channels per round before transaction bytes become the bottleneck

That matters because multilateral clearing eventually wants to compress larger graphs, not just small ones. But the present protocol story should stay grounded:

1. cooperation beats exact per-payment settlement on the hot path
2. cumulative commitments and bundle settlement already prove that
3. BLS is the next multiplier for the multilateral frontier

So when Agon talks about BLS, it should be framed as:

- future signature abstraction
- higher round density
- better multilateral headroom

not as a prerequisite for the protocol to make sense.
