---
title: "Cooperative Scaling"
description: "Why Agon frames repeated machine payments as a clearing problem."
---

Agon treats repeated machine payments as a clearing problem.

When the same counterparties interact many times, exact settlement on every interaction forces each request to carry the same signature, liveness, and fee overhead. In practice, many of those relationships can tolerate deferred settlement as long as obligations are explicit, balances remain non-custodial, and the latest agreed state can be enforced onchain.

Cooperation in Agon has concrete meaning. Participants keep stable identities. Lanes accumulate obligations instead of resetting on every request. Counterparties can decide how much collateral a relationship needs, how frequently it should settle, and how much trust or reputation they are willing to rely on between settlements.

This is why the protocol scales in stages. A single latest commitment already collapses many offchain updates into one settlement. Bundle settlement lets one payee settle many lanes together. Cooperative clearing rounds go further by advancing many lane targets in one shared signed message and applying only residual balance changes.

Multilateral clearing is the densest settlement mode, but it is not the entire thesis. The broader claim is that repeated machine-payment relationships do not need to be modeled as isolated one-off transfers. They can be modeled as long-lived clearing relationships.
