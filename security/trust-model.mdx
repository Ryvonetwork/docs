---
title: "Trust Model"
description: "What Agon assumes and what it does not."
---

Agon is permissionless and non-custodial, but it is not pretending that all payment relationships are identical.

The trust model is:

- protocol rules are public and enforceable onchain
- balances are self-custodied in protocol state, not in an operator database
- counterparties can still choose how much to rely on trust, reputation, or collateral

That makes Agon different from:

- managed micropayment products that still depend on one operator to settle you
- routing networks where every payment pays path-dependent liquidity fees

Agon's claim is that **cooperative deferred settlement** is the right primitive for repeated machine-commerce relationships.
