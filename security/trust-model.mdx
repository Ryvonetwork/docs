---
title: "Trust Model"
description: "What Agon assumes and what it does not."
---

Agon is permissionless and non-custodial, but it does not assume that every payment relationship should be modeled with the same level of trust minimization.

The protocol rules are public and enforceable onchain. Balances live in protocol state rather than in an operator database. At the same time, counterparties can still choose how much a relationship relies on collateral, reputation, or periodic settlement between onchain checkpoints.

That makes Agon different from managed micropayment products that still depend on one operator to settle users, and from routing networks where each payment pays path-dependent liquidity costs. Agon is designed for repeated machine-commerce relationships that want non-custodial settlement without forcing every interaction through the full cost of exact final settlement.
