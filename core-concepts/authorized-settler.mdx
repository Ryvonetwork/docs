---
title: "Authorized Settler"
description: "Why `authorized_settler` exists and what delegated settlement enables in Agon V4."
---

`authorized_settler` exists so a channel can delegate **settlement submission** without changing who signed the payment update.

That distinction is important.

The channel's `authorized_signer` still signs the economic state. `authorized_settler` only adds another key that is allowed to submit that signed state onchain.

## Why This Feature Exists

Many teams do not want to run settlement operations themselves.

They may want an external service to handle:

1. relaying
2. retries
3. batching
4. invoicing
5. compliance checks
6. payment operations in general

`authorized_settler` gives them a native protocol mechanism for that delegation while keeping balances in protocol state.

## What It Means Today

In V4, `authorized_settler` is **additive**, not exclusive.

If a commitment includes `authorized_settler`, then either of these may submit it:

1. the payee owner
2. the delegated settler named in the signed message

That design is intentional. It lets teams use operators without taking away the payee's direct settlement path.

## What It Applies To

`authorized_settler` only applies to unilateral commitment messages:

- `settle_individual`
- `settle_commitment_bundle`

It does not affect cooperative clearing rounds because those rounds are keyed to the signed participant roster, not to a delegated unilateral submitter.

## Minimal Example

```ts
const message = createCommitmentMessage({
  payerId: channel.payerId,
  payeeId: channel.payeeId,
  tokenId: 1,
  committedAmount: nextAmount,
  messageDomain,
  authorizedSettler: operator.publicKey,
});
```

After that message is signed by the channel's `authorized_signer`, either the payee or `operator.publicKey` may submit it.

## Fees Are Separate

`authorized_settler` does not automatically receive a fee.

If the operator should be paid, the commitment must also include:

1. `fee_amount`
2. `fee_recipient_id`

Those fields are independent from `authorized_settler`. A delegated settler can be unpaid, and a payee can submit a message that still routes the fee to an operator participant if that operator was named as the fee recipient in the signed message.

## What It Does Not Do

The current V4 feature is not an escrow primitive.

It does not:

1. give the delegated settler control over the amount
2. make the delegated settler the only party allowed to settle
3. add dispute-resolution logic
4. let an external program control settlement policy onchain

Those ideas may be useful later, but they are separate features. Today `authorized_settler` is best understood as **delegated submission for unilateral settlement**.
