---
title: "Authorized Settler"
description: "Why `authorized_settler` exists and what delegated settlement enables in the current Agon commitment model."
---

`authorized_settler` lets a payment commitment name an additional submitter without changing who signed the payment update.

The distinction is simple:

- `authorized_signer` signs the payment update
- `authorized_settler` can submit that signed update onchain

## Overview

Many teams do not want to run settlement operations themselves. They may want a third party to handle relaying, batching, invoicing, compliance checks, or payment operations.

`authorized_settler` gives them a protocol-level way to delegate submission while keeping balances in protocol state.

## Common Use Cases

Common examples include:

- payment gateways
- invoicing providers
- compliance and KYT services
- operator hubs
- internal payment operations services

If you want the larger network pattern that often sits on top of this feature, read [Operator Hubs](/core-concepts/operator-hubs).

## How It Works

`authorized_settler` is additive, not exclusive.

If a commitment includes `authorized_settler`, then either of these may submit it:

1. the payee owner
2. the delegated settler named in the signed message

This design keeps the payee's direct settlement path intact while still allowing operational delegation.

## Where It Applies

`authorized_settler` only applies to unilateral commitment messages:

- `settle_individual`
- `settle_commitment_bundle`

It does not affect cooperative clearing rounds because those rounds are keyed to the signed participant roster, not to a delegated unilateral submitter.

## Example

```ts
const message = createCommitmentMessage({
  payerId: channel.payerId,
  payeeId: channel.payeeId,
  tokenId: 1,
  committedAmount: nextAmount,
  messageDomain,
  authorizedSettler: operator.publicKey,
});
```

After that message is signed by the channel's `authorized_signer`, either the payee or `operator.publicKey` may submit it.

## Operator Payment Is Separate

`authorized_settler` does not automatically receive a fee.

If the operator should be paid, that payment should be modeled as its own ordinary lane commitment. In practice that means flows such as:

- `payer -> provider` for the service itself
- `provider -> operator` or `payer -> operator` for settlement or coordination services

That keeps operator compensation durable and cumulative instead of embedding it as a one-off field inside the unilateral commitment message.

## Limits

The current feature is not an escrow primitive.

It does not:

1. give the delegated settler control over the amount
2. make the delegated settler the only party allowed to settle
3. add dispute-resolution logic
4. let an external program control settlement policy onchain

Today, `authorized_settler` is best understood as delegated submission for unilateral settlement.
