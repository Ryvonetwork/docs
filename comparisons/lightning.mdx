---
title: "Lightning"
description: "How Agon's cooperative-clearing model differs from Lightning's routing model."
---

Lightning and Agon both move authorization offchain, but they scale in different ways.

Lightning scales through routing, channel liquidity, and path-dependent forwarding fees. Agon scales through cumulative obligations, payee-side bundling, and cooperative clearing.

Lightning is a strong fit when the goal is open, trust-minimized internet money across an arbitrary network. Agon is a stronger fit when the goal is repeated machine-payment relationships that want to compress settlement rather than price every hop in a routing graph.
