---
title: "Circle Nanopayments"
description: "Why Agon positions itself as a protocol rather than a managed micropayment rail."
---

Circle Nanopayments validates the market for offchain authorization and batched settlement.

The difference is where trust and control live.

Agon is designed to be permissionless, non-custodial, protocol-native, and operator-neutral. The central question is not only whether micropayments can be cheap. It is whether they can settle without depending on one operator's continued permission and availability.

Agon's answer is a public clearing protocol rather than a managed payment rail.
